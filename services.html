The Science in more detail Definition of cavitation:

Bubbles of liquid form and grow inside the fat, and inside these bubbles other micro bubbles full of gas are created which then expand, i.e. a gaseous cavity forms inside the liquid bubble.

The pressure exerted on the newly expanded bubbles compresses them, increasing the temperature of the gas contained inside the bubble until it collapses in on itself, imploding and releasing energy on the inside of the fatty material and adipocytes, fluidising it and allowing them to escape.
